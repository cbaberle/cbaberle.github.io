About me

I am a student of Computer Sci­ence and Philosophy at Merton College, University of Oxford, interest­ed in logic, programm­ing languages, category theory, and the foundations of mathematics and computer science.

Very broadly, the goal of my studies is to better understand the nature of inform­ation – what information is, how it can be put to practical use in solving problems, etc. At present, I am pre­occupied with two sub­problems of this underlying theme:

  1. The problem of abstraction: how is it possible for some information to gen­eralise or abstract other inform­ation? What makes such abstract inform­ation useful?
  2. The problem of interaction: what is the correct way to model exchange of inform­ation between an inform­ation-processing system and its environ­ment?

My wager is that the process-oriented view afforded by comput­er science offers new perspectives on these and related questions, which may in turn be profitably applied to the design of novel systems, both digital and concept­ual, for the effective use of inform­ation.

In a former life, I was an aspiring composer, and I still spend much of my spare time writing music; you can find a selection of my compositions here.
